03 · How It Works

Three steps to capture.
Two ways to send.

TraceBrief lives in Chrome's side panel — open while you browse, capture when something breaks, format it instantly. No file management. No tab-switching. No uploading.

01
Capture
Screenshot or Record
Hit capture while the broken thing is still broken. Standard screenshot, full-page scroll, or screen recording for timing and behaviour bugs. Alt+S to capture without leaving your flow.
02
Structure
Add Steps & Context
Log your reproduction steps. Add Expected vs Actual. TraceBrief auto-captures URL, browser, OS, and timestamp. Annotate screenshots with arrows and labels if needed.
03
Send
Copy or Export
Now you've got two clean options — paste directly from the panel, or export a file. Either way, the other person gets everything they need to understand and fix the issue.

08 · Questions

Stuff people ask
before they install.

Is it actually free? What's the catch?
+
Yes, free. No catch. No data harvesting, no ads, no features paywalled later. TraceBrief runs locally. We make money when people find it useful enough to tell their teams. That's the model.
Do I need an account?
+
No. Install it, open it, use it. No email. No password. No "please verify your account." We are not collecting leads. We just want to be a useful tool.
Can I use it for non-technical things?
+
Absolutely. TraceBrief is for anyone who needs to prove what happened on a screen. Netflix customer service, HR reporting a broken payroll portal, a student whose assignment won't submit — the tool doesn't care what industry you're in. If it broke on a screen, you can document it.
Do I have to export a file?
+
No. The copy/paste path is the primary workflow. The panel stays open while you work. Capture everything, then paste the formatted output — Markdown, plain text, CSV, HTML, or JSON — directly into your ticket, email, or Slack. Export a file only when you specifically need an attachment.
Where does my data go? Who sees my screenshots?
+
Your data stays on your machine until you choose to send it. We request only the Chrome permissions needed to function. There is no server ingesting your screenshots. Local-first is the architecture, not a marketing line.
Does it work with Jira / GitHub / Linear / anything?
+
Yes — because we output to plain text, Markdown, CSV, HTML, and JSON, TraceBrief works with anything that accepts pasted text or a file attachment. Which is everything. We don't do fragile API integrations. You copy. You paste. It works.
